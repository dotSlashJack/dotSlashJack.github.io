Who and What?
     I'm Jack, a senior at Emory University finishing a BA in public health and behavioral modeling. I study the healthacre system from an interdisciplinary perspective. My studies have taken me on a journey through the fields healthcare, sociology, biology, linguistics, quantitive sciences, computer science and computational modeling, physics, research and writing, and classical literature. I do public health, but that's only the beginning as you'll see if you read the rest of this page.


Why?

     Due to countless surgeries and doctors office visits, I’ve been personally immersed in the healthcare system from the day I was born. I would constantly imagine ways that the healthcare system could be improved, from better equipment to cures for diseases to improved physician-patient interactions. My motivation to improve healthcare—and more fundamentally improve peoples’ quality of life—has been at the core of everything I’ve done. Eventually, I landed in public health in order to work on this goal.


How?

     As I mentioned above, I’ve studied public health from a variety of perspectives including sociology, coding and emerging technology, biology, physics, behavioral modeling, computational linguistics, marketing and PR, and data visualization. Even more critically, however, I like to ask lots of questions. Here are a few I’m currently working on, or am at least considering:

  • How much access to healthcare records should patients have? How do we keep them secure and disperse them (using EmTech i.e. blockchain)?
  • How can data be visualized to make it easier to understand for both patients and doctors?
  • Who are the major stakeholders in facilitating changes to the healthcare system? What are they motivated by? How can data be effectively communicated to them in a persuasive way?
  • How can healthcare record entry be simplified and/or sped up? How can it be automatically stored and processed to display insights about treatments over time?
  • How is the healthcare system being decentralized away from the current hospital-centric model? What impact does this have on healthcare accessibility and cost?
  • What is the return on investment for a given public health intervention? How is this determined? Who wants to know?
  • How can data be effectively generated and shared inside (and outside) the healthcare system (including public health departments, hosptials, inependent researchers, etc.)?
  • What are the major barriers to patients changing their behaviors towards healthier ones? How can geographic barriers be overcome?
  • Who are the biggest players in healthcare reform going to be? What is the role of private corporations going to look like?
  • What insights can behavioral economics provide to those working in healthcare? How about models from (unexpected?) fields like physics?
Would you like to collaborate with me on any of these? Please contact me!
